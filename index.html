Jhené Aiko

Jhené Aiko is an amarican R&B singer-songwriter and rapper. She was born on march 16, 1988 and began her music career in 2002 as a backing vocalist for the R&B band B2K. In March of 2011, Jhené Aiko realeased her first full-length album, labeled Sailing Souls. In december of that year she signed a contract with producer No I.D.'s record label ARTium Recordings. She, alongside Lil Wayne co-performed on Big Sean's single "Beware" in 2013 which became her first top 40 single on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. Her second and third albums, Trip (2017) and Chilombo (2020), where she spiked up to number two on the Billboard 200.

She had many achievments, some of which include;

  • Jhené Aiko is one of the few artist int his day to use her voice for social activism and advocacy.
  • Two of her albums,Trip (2017) and Chilombo (2020) earned her three Grammy Award nominations, including Album of the year.
  • Jhené Aiko shares her thoughts, feelings and experiences through her music.
